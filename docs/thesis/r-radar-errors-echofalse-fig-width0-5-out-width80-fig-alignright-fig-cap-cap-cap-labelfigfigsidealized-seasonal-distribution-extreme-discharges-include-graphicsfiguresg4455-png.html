A weather radar transmits a signal along a path called the radar beam, and the antenna rotates at a constant elevation angle to complete one sweep or elevation scan. The antenna makes a series of sweeps at increasing elevation angles, producing a set of nesting conical surfaces of three-dimensional data called a volume scan. When the radar beams encounter a backscattering target (e.g.Â rain drops, hail, snow, birds), some of the energy is scattered back to the radar receiver, and is then interpreted as the quantity reflectivity factor.
